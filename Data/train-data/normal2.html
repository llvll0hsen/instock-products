About this item

Featuring a stunning Ultra HD picture and premium Smart TV experience optimized for Ultra HD streaming; the VIZIO M-Series Ultra HD Full-Array LED Smart TV turns any room into a captivating world where picture is everything!

This product qualifies for Free Premium Delivery.
How it works: A carrier unpacks TV in room of choice, checks for damage and removes packaging when done. TVs sold through Marketplace are not eligible for Premium Delivery.

  • 80" LCD panel
    With a 3840 x 2160 Ultra HD resolution (4K x 2K)

5.0 stars 6/23/2015 by jh20001
by jh20001

My neighbor & good friend has been considering an 80” ever since they saw ours since last year. I didn’t expect him to outshine us as well though. He just bought this one a few days ago. When we first dropped our eyes on it, I was surprised that it instantly made my Vizio look outdated. Jealous I was, yes! I wound up looking it up to see the specs and I think they have done their homework at throwing punches at this 4K world that is developing. I never thought they would be this cheap already. It runs nearly the same price I paid for my 80-in 1080. So far there doesn’t seem to be a lot of feedback. The only review I saw [outside of some preview topics] was http://pocinc.net/blog/product-reviews/review-vizios-m80-c3-80-inch-affordable-4k-ultra-hd-smart-tv . Most of the other topics just spoke about what’s to come, more than actually using it like we did at my friends. If I knew the price would be so cheap so quick, I may have hesitated a little for crying out loud. I am happy with my 1080 though. Maybe I the future we can justify upgrading, but even though it is cheap[er] than some of the others, you can only justify spending the money so many times in life. I can say though that I recommend it. Unless you are oddly looking to spend closure to 10-25 grand, this is most likely your best bet into 4K. We will be stopping by tomorrow for a movie and popcorn. The movie Interstellar [should be a blast on it].
